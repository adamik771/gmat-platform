## Q1
**difficulty:** Easy
**type:** Problem Solving
**topic:** Basic Percents

What is 35% of 80?

- A) 24
- B) 26
- C) 28
- D) 32
- E) 35

**answer:** C
**explanation:** Translate directly: 35% of 80 = 0.35 x 80 = 28. A faster route: 10% of 80 is 8, so 30% is 24 and 5% is 4, giving 24 + 4 = 28. Breaking percents into 10% and 5% chunks is almost always faster than grinding decimals.

## Q2
**difficulty:** Easy
**type:** Problem Solving
**topic:** Basic Percents

If 18 is x percent of 60, what is the value of x?

- A) 18
- B) 24
- C) 30
- D) 36
- E) 42

**answer:** C
**explanation:** Translate "18 is x percent of 60" as 18 = (x/100)(60), so x = 1800/60 = 30. Alternatively, the part-over-whole approach: 18/60 = 3/10 = 30%. Recognizing common fractions as percents (1/4 = 25%, 3/10 = 30%, 3/8 = 37.5%) saves time.

## Q3
**difficulty:** Easy
**type:** Problem Solving
**topic:** Ratios

A box contains only red and green pens in the ratio 4:7. If there are 44 pens in total, how many green pens are in the box?

- A) 16
- B) 22
- C) 24
- D) 28
- E) 32

**answer:** D
**explanation:** The ratio parts sum to 4 + 7 = 11, so each part equals 44/11 = 4 pens. Green pens = 7 x 4 = 28. Trap A (16) is the count of red pens. Always confirm which quantity the question actually asks for before picking an answer.

## Q4
**difficulty:** Easy
**type:** Problem Solving
**topic:** Percent Change

A jacket originally priced at $120 is marked down by 25%. What is the sale price?

- A) $80
- B) $90
- C) $95
- D) $96
- E) $100

**answer:** B
**explanation:** A 25% discount means the shopper pays 75% of the original price: 0.75 x 120 = 90. The "multiplier" method (multiply by 0.75 once) is cleaner than subtracting 25% of 120 = 30 from 120. Use multipliers anytime you see a single percent change.

## Q5
**difficulty:** Easy
**type:** Problem Solving
**topic:** Proportions

On a map, 2 inches represents 15 actual miles. If two cities are 7 inches apart on the map, how many actual miles apart are they?

- A) 30
- B) 45
- C) 52.5
- D) 60
- E) 105

**answer:** C
**explanation:** Set up the proportion 2/15 = 7/m, so m = 15 x 7 / 2 = 105/2 = 52.5 miles. A common trap is E (105), which forgets to divide by 2. Label units (inches on top, miles on bottom) on both sides to avoid flipping the ratio.

## Q6
**difficulty:** Easy
**type:** Problem Solving
**topic:** Basic Percents

In a class of 40 students, 14 are left-handed. What percent of the class is left-handed?

- A) 28%
- B) 32%
- C) 35%
- D) 38%
- E) 40%

**answer:** C
**explanation:** Percent = part/whole x 100 = 14/40 x 100 = 35%. You can also simplify 14/40 = 7/20, and 7/20 = 35/100 = 35%. Memorizing that /20 fractions convert to percents by multiplying numerator by 5 is a time saver.

## Q7
**difficulty:** Medium
**type:** Problem Solving
**topic:** Percent Change

The price of a stock increases by 20% in January, then decreases by 20% in February. By what percent has the stock's price changed from the start of January to the end of February?

- A) It decreased by 4%
- B) It decreased by 2%
- C) It is unchanged
- D) It increased by 2%
- E) It increased by 4%

**answer:** A
**explanation:** Successive percent changes do NOT cancel. Use smart numbers: start at $100. After +20% it's $120; after -20% of $120 it drops by $24 to $96. That's a 4% decrease from $100. The general rule: a +x% then -x% change always ends below the start by (x/10)² percent.

## Q8
**difficulty:** Medium
**type:** Problem Solving
**topic:** Mixtures

A chemist has 30 liters of a 20% salt solution. How many liters of pure water must be added to produce a 15% salt solution?

- A) 5
- B) 7.5
- C) 10
- D) 12
- E) 15

**answer:** C
**explanation:** The salt doesn't change when you add water — only the total volume grows. Salt = 0.20 x 30 = 6 liters. After adding w liters of water, 6/(30 + w) = 0.15, so 30 + w = 40, giving w = 10. Lock onto the quantity that stays constant (salt here) — that's the anchor in nearly every mixture problem.

## Q9
**difficulty:** Medium
**type:** Problem Solving
**topic:** Profit/Markup/Discount

A retailer marks up a product by 40% over cost, then offers a 25% discount off the marked price. If the cost is $80, what is the retailer's profit on a sale?

- A) $4
- B) $8
- C) $12
- D) $16
- E) $20

**answer:** A
**explanation:** Marked price = 80 x 1.40 = 112. Sale price = 112 x 0.75 = 84. Profit = 84 - 80 = 4. The combined multiplier is 1.40 x 0.75 = 1.05, meaning the net effect is just a 5% markup over cost — which equals $4 on an $80 item. Convert each percent step to a multiplier and chain them.

## Q10
**difficulty:** Medium
**type:** Problem Solving
**topic:** Ratios

The ratio of a to b is 3:5 and the ratio of b to c is 4:7. What is the ratio of a to c?

- A) 3:7
- B) 12:35
- C) 15:28
- D) 12:28
- E) 21:20

**answer:** B
**explanation:** Chain the ratios by making the common term match. Scale a:b = 3:5 to 12:20 and b:c = 4:7 to 20:35 (multiply first by 4, second by 5 so b = 20 in both). Now a:b:c = 12:20:35, so a:c = 12:35. Whenever you chain ratios, the shared variable must have identical values in both ratios before you combine.

## Q11
**difficulty:** Medium
**type:** Problem Solving
**topic:** Percent Change

A company's revenue grew by 50% from 2023 to 2024. If 2024 revenue was $900,000, what was the 2023 revenue?

- A) $450,000
- B) $540,000
- C) $600,000
- D) $675,000
- E) $750,000

**answer:** C
**explanation:** A 50% increase means 2024 revenue = 1.5 x (2023 revenue). So 2023 = 900,000/1.5 = 600,000. Trap A divides by 2 (treating 50% increase as if "half was added"); trap B subtracts 50% of 900,000. When reversing a percent increase, always divide by the multiplier — never subtract the percent.

## Q12
**difficulty:** Medium
**type:** Problem Solving
**topic:** Mixtures

A 12-liter mixture contains alcohol and water in the ratio 1:3. How many liters of alcohol must be added so that the ratio of alcohol to water becomes 2:3?

- A) 2
- B) 3
- C) 4
- D) 5
- E) 6

**answer:** B
**explanation:** Initial alcohol = (1/4)(12) = 3 liters; water = 9 liters. Water is unchanged, so in the new mix alcohol/water = 2/3 means alcohol = (2/3)(9) = 6 liters. Added alcohol = 6 - 3 = 3. Lock onto the unchanging quantity (water), then scale the ratio.

## Q13
**difficulty:** Medium
**type:** Problem Solving
**topic:** Basic Percents

If 30% of x equals 45% of y, what is the ratio of x to y?

- A) 2:3
- B) 3:2
- C) 1:2
- D) 2:1
- E) 3:4

**answer:** B
**explanation:** 0.30x = 0.45y, so x/y = 0.45/0.30 = 3/2. Whenever the smaller percent applies to the larger quantity, that quantity is larger — here x (with only 30%) must exceed y (with 45%) to keep the products equal. This "inverse" intuition eliminates trap A quickly.

## Q14
**difficulty:** Medium
**type:** Data Sufficiency
**topic:** Ratios

In a bag of marbles containing only red and blue marbles, what is the number of red marbles?

(1) The ratio of red marbles to blue marbles is 3:5.
(2) There are 24 more blue marbles than red marbles.

- A) Statement (1) ALONE is sufficient, but statement (2) ALONE is not sufficient.
- B) Statement (2) ALONE is sufficient, but statement (1) ALONE is not sufficient.
- C) BOTH statements TOGETHER are sufficient, but NEITHER statement ALONE is sufficient.
- D) EACH statement ALONE is sufficient.
- E) Statements (1) and (2) TOGETHER are NOT sufficient.

**answer:** C
**explanation:** Statement (1) gives only the ratio — red could be 3, 6, 9, etc. — insufficient. Statement (2) gives a difference but no ratio — insufficient. Together: red = 3k, blue = 5k, and 5k - 3k = 24, so k = 12 and red = 36. Unique answer — sufficient. A ratio plus any one absolute count (total, part, or difference) always determines both parts.

## Q15
**difficulty:** Medium
**type:** Data Sufficiency
**topic:** Percent Change

A store increased the price of a television by some percent. What was the final price?

(1) The price was increased by 15%.
(2) If the price had been increased by $45 more, the total increase would have been 25%.

- A) Statement (1) ALONE is sufficient, but statement (2) ALONE is not sufficient.
- B) Statement (2) ALONE is sufficient, but statement (1) ALONE is not sufficient.
- C) BOTH statements TOGETHER are sufficient, but NEITHER statement ALONE is sufficient.
- D) EACH statement ALONE is sufficient.
- E) Statements (1) and (2) TOGETHER are NOT sufficient.

**answer:** C
**explanation:** Statement (1) gives only the percent — no original price, insufficient. Statement (2) alone ties the actual increase to a 25% benchmark but fixes neither P nor the actual percent — insufficient. Combined: let P be the original price. Then 0.15P + 45 = 0.25P, so 0.10P = 45 and P = 450; final = 450 x 1.15 = 517.50. A percent relationship plus one dollar anchor is the classic "together sufficient" DS pattern.

## Q16
**difficulty:** Hard
**type:** Problem Solving
**topic:** Mixtures

A 50-liter mixture is 40% alcohol. A second mixture, which is 70% alcohol, is added to the first until the resulting mixture is 55% alcohol. How many liters of the second mixture were added?

- A) 25
- B) 40
- C) 50
- D) 60
- E) 75

**answer:** C
**explanation:** Let x be liters of the 70% mixture added. Total alcohol: 0.40(50) + 0.70x = 0.55(50 + x), so 20 + 0.7x = 27.5 + 0.55x, giving 0.15x = 7.5 and x = 50. Shortcut (alligation): the target 55% is 15 away from 40% and 15 away from 70% — equal distances mean equal volumes, confirming x = 50. Alligation is lightning fast once you see the distances are equal.

## Q17
**difficulty:** Hard
**type:** Problem Solving
**topic:** Profit/Markup/Discount

A merchant sells a watch for $140 and earns a 30% profit on the selling price. If instead the watch had been sold at a 30% profit on the cost price, what would the selling price have been?

- A) $122.20
- B) $124.60
- C) $125.80
- D) $127.40
- E) $130.00

**answer:** D
**explanation:** "30% profit on selling price" means cost = 70% of selling price = 0.70 x 140 = 98. A 30% profit on cost gives selling price = 1.30 x 98 = 127.40. The trap is treating the two phrases as equivalent — they aren't: "margin on price" and "markup on cost" use different bases, so always identify the base before multiplying.

## Q18
**difficulty:** Hard
**type:** Data Sufficiency
**topic:** Mixtures

A tank contains a mixture of water and alcohol. What percent of the mixture is alcohol?

(1) If 10 liters of water were added, the mixture would be 20% alcohol.
(2) If 5 liters of alcohol were added, the mixture would be 40% alcohol.

- A) Statement (1) ALONE is sufficient, but statement (2) ALONE is not sufficient.
- B) Statement (2) ALONE is sufficient, but statement (1) ALONE is not sufficient.
- C) BOTH statements TOGETHER are sufficient, but NEITHER statement ALONE is sufficient.
- D) EACH statement ALONE is sufficient.
- E) Statements (1) and (2) TOGETHER are NOT sufficient.

**answer:** C
**explanation:** Let a = liters alcohol, t = total liters. We want a/t. Statement (1): a/(t+10) = 0.20 — one equation, two unknowns, insufficient. Statement (2): (a+5)/(t+5) = 0.40 — insufficient. Combined: two linear equations in a and t solve uniquely (a = 7, t = 25, so alcohol is 28%). When a percent problem hides two unknowns, you need two independent equations — not just two facts.
