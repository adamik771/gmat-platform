## Q1
**difficulty:** Easy
**type:** Problem Solving
**topic:** Rate/Time/Distance

A car travels at 60 miles per hour for 2.5 hours. How many miles does it travel?

- A) 120
- B) 130
- C) 140
- D) 150
- E) 160

**answer:** D
**explanation:** Distance = Rate x Time = 60 x 2.5 = 150 miles.

## Q2
**difficulty:** Easy
**type:** Problem Solving
**topic:** Profit/Loss

A merchant buys a product for $40 and sells it for $52. What is the merchant's profit as a percentage of the cost?

- A) 12%
- B) 20%
- C) 23%
- D) 25%
- E) 30%

**answer:** E
**explanation:** Profit = $52 - $40 = $12. Profit percentage = (12/40) x 100 = 30%. Trap answer A gives the dollar profit rather than the percentage.

## Q3

**type:** Problem Solving
**topic:** Age Problems

Maria is currently 3 times as old as her daughter. If her daughter is 8 years old, how old will Maria be in 6 years?

- A) 24
- B) 27
- C) 30
- D) 32
- E) 36

**answer:** C
**explanation:** Maria's current age = 3 x 8 = 24. In 6 years, Maria will be 24 + 6 = 30. Trap answer A is Maria's current age, not her future age.

## Q5
**difficulty:** Medium
**type:** Problem Solving
**topic:** Rate/Time/Distance

Train A leaves Station X heading east at 70 mph. Two hours later, Train B leaves Station X heading east at 90 mph. How many hours after Train B departs will it overtake Train A?

- A) 5
- B) 6
- C) 7
- D) 8
- E) 9

**answer:** C
**explanation:** When Train B departs, Train A has already traveled 70 x 2 = 140 miles. Train B gains on Train A at a rate of 90 - 70 = 20 mph. Time for Train B to close the 140-mile gap = 140/20 = 7 hours.

## Q6
**difficulty:** Medium
**type:** Problem Solving
**topic:** Work Rate

Machine A can complete a job in 6 hours. Machine B can complete the same job in 12 hours. If both machines work together, how many hours will it take to complete the job?

- A) 3
- B) 4
- C) 5
- D) 6
- E) 9

**answer:** B
**explanation:** Machine A's rate = 1/6 of the job per hour. Machine B's rate = 1/12 of the job per hour. Combined rate = 1/6 + 1/12 = 2/12 + 1/12 = 3/12 = 1/4 of the job per hour. Time = 1/(1/4) = 4 hours. Trap answer E (9) is the simple average of 6 and 12.

## Q7
**difficulty:** Medium
**type:** Problem Solving
**topic:** Mixture

A chemist has 10 liters of a 30% acid solution. How many liters of pure acid must be added to create a 50% acid solution?

- A) 2
- B) 3
- C) 4
- D) 5
- E) 6

**answer:** C
**explanation:** The 10-liter solution contains 0.30 x 10 = 3 liters of acid. Let x = liters of pure acid added. The new solution has (3 + x) liters of acid in (10 + x) liters total. Setting up the equation: (3 + x)/(10 + x) = 0.50. So 3 + x = 5 + 0.5x, giving 0.5x = 2, and x = 4 liters.

## Q8
**difficulty:** Medium
**type:** Problem Solving
**topic:** Profit/Loss

A retailer marks up the cost of goods by 40% and then offers a seasonal discount of 10% on the marked price. What is the retailer's profit as a percentage of cost?

- A) 20%
- B) 24%
- C) 26%
- D) 30%
- E) 36%

**answer:** C
**explanation:** Let cost = $100. Marked price = $100 x 1.40 = $140. After 10% discount: $140 x 0.90 = $126. Profit = $126 - $100 = $26. Profit percentage = (26/100) x 100 = 26%.

## Q9
**difficulty:** Hard
**type:** Problem Solving
**topic:** Rate/Time/Distance

A cyclist travels from Town A to Town B at 15 mph and returns by the same route at 10 mph. What is the cyclist's average speed, in miles per hour, for the entire round trip?

- A) 11
- B) 12
- C) 12.5
- D) 13
- E) 13.5

**answer:** B
**explanation:** Let the one-way distance be d miles. Time going = d/15. Time returning = d/10. Total distance = 2d. Total time = d/15 + d/10 = 2d/30 + 3d/30 = 5d/30 = d/6. Average speed = Total distance / Total time = 2d / (d/6) = 12 mph. Trap answer C (12.5) comes from simply averaging 15 and 10.

## Q10
**difficulty:** Hard
**type:** Problem Solving
**topic:** Work Rate

Pipe A can fill a tank in 4 hours, and Pipe B can fill the same tank in 6 hours. Pipe C can drain the full tank in 12 hours. If all three pipes are open simultaneously, how many hours will it take to fill the empty tank?

- A) 2
- B) 3
- C) 4
- D) 5
- E) 6

**answer:** B
**explanation:** Rate of Pipe A = 1/4 (filling). Rate of Pipe B = 1/6 (filling). Rate of Pipe C = -1/12 (draining). Combined rate = 1/4 + 1/6 - 1/12 = 3/12 + 2/12 - 1/12 = 4/12 = 1/3. Time to fill = 1/(1/3) = 3 hours.

## Q11
**difficulty:** Hard
**type:** Problem Solving
**topic:** Mixture

A grocer mixes Brand A coffee at $12 per pound with Brand B coffee at $8 per pound to create a blend that sells at $9.50 per pound. If the grocer makes 40 pounds of the blend, how many pounds of Brand A coffee are used?

- A) 10
- B) 12
- C) 15
- D) 18
- E) 20

**answer:** C
**explanation:** Let a = pounds of Brand A and b = pounds of Brand B. Then a + b = 40 and 12a + 8b = 9.50 x 40 = 380. From the first equation, b = 40 - a. Substituting: 12a + 8(40 - a) = 380, so 12a + 320 - 8a = 380, giving 4a = 60, and a = 15 pounds.

## Q12
**difficulty:** Hard
**type:** Problem Solving
**topic:** Age Problems

Five years ago, James was 4 times as old as his nephew. In 3 years, James will be twice as old as his nephew. How old is James now?

- A) 21
- B) 25
- C) 29
- D) 33
- E) 37

**answer:** A
**explanation:** Let J = James's current age and N = nephew's current age. Five years ago: J - 5 = 4(N - 5), giving J = 4N - 15 ... (1). In 3 years: J + 3 = 2(N + 3), giving J = 2N + 3 ... (2). Setting (1) = (2): 4N - 15 = 2N + 3, so 2N = 18, and N = 9. Then J = 2(9) + 3 = 21. Verification: Five years ago, James was 16 and his nephew was 4; 16 = 4 x 4. In 3 years, James will be 24 and his nephew will be 12; 24 = 2 x 12. Both conditions are satisfied.
