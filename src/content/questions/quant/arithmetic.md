## Q1
**difficulty:** Easy
**type:** Problem Solving
**topic:** Fractions

What is the value of 3/4 + 5/8 - 1/2?

- A) 3/8
- B) 5/8
- C) 7/8
- D) 1
- E) 9/8

**answer:** C
**explanation:** Convert all fractions to eighths: 3/4 = 6/8, 5/8 = 5/8, 1/2 = 4/8. So 6/8 + 5/8 - 4/8 = 7/8.

## Q2
**difficulty:** Easy
**type:** Problem Solving
**topic:** Percentages

A shirt originally priced at $80 is discounted by 15%. What is the sale price of the shirt?

- A) $12
- B) $65
- C) $68
- D) $72
- E) $76

**answer:** C
**explanation:** The discount is 15% of $80 = 0.15 x 80 = $12. The sale price is $80 - $12 = $68. Trap answer A is the discount amount itself, not the final price.

## Q3

The ratio of red marbles to blue marbles in a jar is 3:5. If there are 40 marbles in total, how many red marbles are there?

- A) 8
- B) 12
- C) 15
- D) 20
- E) 25

**answer:** C
**explanation:** The total parts in the ratio are 3 + 5 = 8. Each part represents 40/8 = 5 marbles. Red marbles = 3 x 5 = 15. Trap answer E (25) is the number of blue marbles.

## Q4
**difficulty:** Easy
**type:** Problem Solving
**topic:** Decimals

Which of the following is equal to 0.125 x 0.4?

- A) 0.005
- B) 0.05
- C) 0.5
- D) 0.0125
- E) 0.525

**answer:** B
**explanation:** 0.125 x 0.4 = 125 x 4 / 10,000 = 500 / 10,000 = 0.05. Alternatively, 0.125 = 1/8 and 0.4 = 2/5, so (1/8)(2/5) = 2/40 = 1/20 = 0.05.

## Q5
**difficulty:** Medium
**type:** Problem Solving
**topic:** Percentages

A store raises the price of an item by 20% and then offers a 20% discount on the new price. The final price is what percent of the original price?

- A) 92%
- B) 96%
- C) 100%
- D) 104%
- E) 108%

**answer:** B
**explanation:** Let the original price be $100. After a 20% increase, the price becomes $120. A 20% discount on $120 is 0.20 x 120 = $24 off, making the final price $120 - $24 = $96. This is 96% of the original price. The common trap is to think the increase and decrease cancel out (answer C).

## Q6
**difficulty:** Medium
**type:** Problem Solving
**topic:** Powers and Roots

If 2^x = 32 and 3^y = 81, what is the value of x + y?

- A) 7
- B) 8
- C) 9
- D) 10
- E) 12

**answer:** C
**explanation:** 2^x = 32 = 2⁵, so x = 5. 3^y = 81 = 3⁴, so y = 4. Therefore x + y = 5 + 4 = 9.

A recipe calls for flour, sugar, and butter in the ratio 5:3:2. If a baker uses 750 grams of flour, how many grams of butter does the recipe require?

- A) 150
- B) 200
- C) 300
- D) 450
- E) 500

**answer:** C
**explanation:** The ratio of flour to butter is 5:2. If flour = 750 grams, then each part = 750/5 = 150 grams. Butter = 2 x 150 = 300 grams. Trap answer D (450) is the amount of sugar (3 x 150).

## Q8
**difficulty:** Medium
**type:** Problem Solving
**topic:** Absolute Value

If |2x - 7| = 11, what is the sum of all possible values of x?

- A) -2
- B) 5
- C) 7
- D) 9
- E) 11

**answer:** C
**explanation:** Case 1: 2x - 7 = 11, so 2x = 18, x = 9. Case 2: 2x - 7 = -11, so 2x = -4, x = -2. The sum of all possible values is 9 + (-2) = 7.

## Q9
**difficulty:** Hard
**type:** Problem Solving
**topic:** Percentages

In a certain town, 60% of the adults are women. Of the women, 30% are employed full-time. Of the men, 50% are employed full-time. What percent of all adults in the town are employed full-time?

- A) 32%
- B) 35%
- C) 38%
- D) 40%
- E) 42%

**answer:** C
**explanation:** Assume there are 100 adults. Women = 60, Men = 40. Women employed full-time = 0.30 x 60 = 18. Men employed full-time = 0.50 x 40 = 20. Total employed full-time = 18 + 20 = 38. As a percentage of 100 adults, that is 38%. Trap answer D comes from averaging 30% and 50% without weighting.

## Q10
**difficulty:** Hard
**type:** Problem Solving
**topic:** Powers and Roots

What is the value of (27^(2/3) x 16^(3/4)) / (4^(1/2) x 9^(1/2))?

- A) 6
- B) 8
- C) 10
- D) 12
- E) 18

**answer:** D
**explanation:** Evaluate each term: 27^(2/3) = (3³)^(2/3) = 3² = 9. 16^(3/4) = (2⁴)^(3/4) = 2³ = 8. 4^(1/2) = 2. 9^(1/2) = 3. Numerator = 9 x 8 = 72. Denominator = 2 x 3 = 6. Result = 72/6 = 12.

## Q11
**difficulty:** Hard
**type:** Problem Solving
**topic:** Fractions

If 1/a + 1/b = 5/6 and a + b = 10, which of the following is the value of ab?

- A) 6
- B) 10
- C) 12
- D) 15
- E) 20

**answer:** C
**explanation:** Since 1/a + 1/b = (a + b)/(ab) = 5/6, and a + b = 10, we get 10/(ab) = 5/6. Cross-multiplying: 5(ab) = 60, so ab = 12. Trap answer A (6) comes from the denominator of 5/6. Trap answer B (10) comes from the sum a + b.

## Q12
**difficulty:** Hard
**type:** Problem Solving
**topic:** Ratios

A solution contains water and alcohol in the ratio 4:1. If 5 liters of alcohol are added to 25 liters of this solution, what is the new ratio of water to alcohol?

- A) 1:1
- B) 2:1
- C) 3:2
- D) 4:3
- E) 5:3

**answer:** B
**explanation:** In 25 liters of solution with a 4:1 ratio, water = (4/5) x 25 = 20 liters and alcohol = (1/5) x 25 = 5 liters. After adding 5 liters of alcohol: water = 20 liters, alcohol = 5 + 5 = 10 liters. The new ratio of water to alcohol = 20:10 = 2:1.

Which of the following is the simplified form of 42/98?

- A) 2/7
- B) 3/7
- C) 4/7
- D) 6/14
- E) 21/49

**answer:** B
**explanation:** Find the greatest common factor of 42 and 98. Both are divisible by 14: 42/14 = 3 and 98/14 = 7, so 42/98 = 3/7. Trap answer D and E are equivalent to the original but not simplified — on the GMAT, "simplified" means fully reduced.

## Q14

When 0.0837 is rounded to the nearest hundredth, what is the result?

- A) 0.08
- B) 0.083
- C) 0.084
- D) 0.09
- E) 0.1

**answer:** A
**explanation:** The hundredths digit is the second digit after the decimal point, which is 8. Look at the next digit (3) to decide rounding — since 3 is less than 5, round down, keeping 0.08. Trap answer D comes from incorrectly rounding up because of the non-zero digits further right; only the digit immediately after the rounding place matters.

## Q15
**difficulty:** Medium
**type:** Problem Solving
**topic:** Fractions

What is the value of (1 + 1/2) / (1 - 1/3)?

- A) 3/4
- B) 9/8
- C) 5/4
- D) 9/4
- E) 5/2

**answer:** D
**explanation:** Simplify the numerator: 1 + 1/2 = 3/2. Simplify the denominator: 1 - 1/3 = 2/3. Dividing fractions means multiplying by the reciprocal: (3/2) / (2/3) = (3/2) x (3/2) = 9/4. Complex fractions become easy once you resolve the top and bottom independently before dividing.

## Q16
**difficulty:** Medium
**type:** Problem Solving
**topic:** Order of Operations

What is the value of 8 - 2 x (3 - 5)² + 12 / 4?

- A) -5
- B) 1
- C) 3
- D) 7
- E) 19

**answer:** C
**explanation:** Apply PEMDAS strictly. Parentheses first: (3 - 5) = -2. Exponent: (-2)² = 4. Now: 8 - 2 x 4 + 12/4 = 8 - 8 + 3 = 3. The most common trap is to compute 8 - 2 = 6 first, which violates order of operations — multiplication and division bind tighter than subtraction.

## Q17
**difficulty:** Medium
**type:** Problem Solving
**topic:** Absolute Value

If x = -3 and y = 2, what is the value of |x - y| - |y - x| + |x| - |y|?

- A) -5
- B) -1
- C) 0
- D) 1
- E) 5

**answer:** D
**explanation:** Note that |x - y| always equals |y - x| because absolute value ignores sign, so those two terms cancel. The expression reduces to |x| - |y| = |-3| - |2| = 3 - 2 = 1. Recognizing the cancellation saves time; plugging in directly also works but is slower.

## Q18
**difficulty:** Medium
**type:** Data Sufficiency
**topic:** Fractions

Is the fraction p/q greater than 1/2?

(1) p + q = 20
(2) q = 2p - 5

- A) Statement (1) ALONE is sufficient, but statement (2) alone is not sufficient.
- B) Statement (2) ALONE is sufficient, but statement (1) alone is not sufficient.
- C) BOTH statements TOGETHER are sufficient, but NEITHER statement ALONE is sufficient.
- D) EACH statement ALONE is sufficient.
- E) Statements (1) and (2) TOGETHER are NOT sufficient.

**answer:** B
**explanation:** p/q > 1/2 is equivalent to 2p > q (assuming q > 0). Statement (1) gives p + q = 20 but p and q could be (5, 15) giving p/q = 1/3, or (15, 5) giving p/q = 3 — insufficient. Statement (2) gives q = 2p - 5, so 2p - q = 5 > 0, meaning 2p > q, so p/q > 1/2 — sufficient. Watch the sign of q assumption: DS problems on the GMAT typically supply positive values, and statement (2) forces p > 2.5 for q to stay positive.

## Q19
**difficulty:** Hard
**type:** Problem Solving
**topic:** Fractions

Which of the following fractions is closest to 1/3?

- A) 7/22
- B) 11/32
- C) 5/16
- D) 9/28
- E) 13/40

**answer:** B
**explanation:** Compare each fraction to 1/3 by cross-multiplying against 1/3. For 11/32 vs 1/3: 3 x 11 = 33 vs 1 x 32 = 32, so 11/32 exceeds 1/3 by 1/(3 x 32) = 1/96. For 7/22 vs 1/3: 21 vs 22, so 7/22 is below 1/3 by 1/(3 x 22) = 1/66. Compute all five gaps this way and the smallest is 1/96, belonging to 11/32. The shortcut: the fraction whose "cross-multiplication gap" produces the smallest reciprocal-style difference wins — you never need to convert to decimals.

## Q20
**difficulty:** Hard
**type:** Data Sufficiency
**topic:** Arithmetic Sequences

The sum of the first n terms of a certain sequence equals 3n² + 2n. What is the value of the 10th term?

(1) The first term of the sequence is 5.
(2) The common difference between consecutive terms is 6.

- A) Statement (1) ALONE is sufficient, but statement (2) alone is not sufficient.
- B) Statement (2) ALONE is sufficient, but statement (1) alone is not sufficient.
- C) BOTH statements TOGETHER are sufficient, but NEITHER statement ALONE is sufficient.
- D) EACH statement ALONE is sufficient.
- E) Statements (1) and (2) TOGETHER are NOT sufficient.

**answer:** D
**explanation:** The stem already fully determines the sequence: the nth term equals S_n - S_(n-1) = (3n² + 2n) - (3(n-1)² + 2(n-1)) = 6n - 1. So the 10th term is 59 regardless of the statements. Whenever the stem alone answers the question, both statements are individually sufficient (they're consistent with the stem: first term = 5, common difference = 6). This is a classic GMAT trap — always test whether the question is answerable from the stem before evaluating statements.
