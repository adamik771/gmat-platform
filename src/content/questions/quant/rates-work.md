## Q1
**difficulty:** Easy
**type:** Problem Solving
**topic:** Distance-Rate-Time

A cyclist rides at a constant speed of 18 km/h. How far will the cyclist travel in 40 minutes?

- A) 9 km
- B) 10 km
- C) 11 km
- D) 12 km
- E) 15 km

**answer:** D
**explanation:** D = RT, but time must match the rate's unit. Convert 40 minutes to 40/60 = 2/3 hour. Distance = 18 x 2/3 = 12 km. Trap answer E comes from forgetting to convert and computing 18 x (40/48) or similar.

## Q2
**difficulty:** Easy
**type:** Problem Solving
**topic:** Combined Work

Pipe A can fill a tank in 6 hours and pipe B can fill the same tank in 4 hours. If both pipes are opened together, how long will it take to fill the tank?

- A) 1.6 hours
- B) 2 hours
- C) 2.4 hours
- D) 3 hours
- E) 5 hours

**answer:** C
**explanation:** Combined work problems use the rate formula: 1/a + 1/b = 1/T. So 1/6 + 1/4 = 2/12 + 3/12 = 5/12. Therefore T = 12/5 = 2.4 hours. Never average the times (that gives 5, trap E).

A train travels 210 miles in 3.5 hours. What is its average speed in miles per hour?

- A) 55
- B) 58

## Q4
**difficulty:** Easy
**type:** Problem Solving
**topic:** Combined Work

Maria can paint a room in 5 hours. John can paint the same room in 5 hours. Working together, how long will it take them?

- A) 2 hours
- B) 2.5 hours
- C) 3 hours
- D) 5 hours
- E) 10 hours

**answer:** B
**explanation:** When two workers have the same individual time t, together they take t/2. Check with the formula: 1/5 + 1/5 = 2/5, so T = 5/2 = 2.5 hours. Doubling workers halves the time.

## Q5
**difficulty:** Easy
**type:** Problem Solving
**topic:** Two Objects Moving

Two cars start from the same point and drive in opposite directions. One drives at 50 km/h and the other at 70 km/h. After how many hours will they be 360 km apart?

- A) 2
- B) 2.5
- C) 3
- D) 3.5
- E) 4

**answer:** C
**explanation:** When two objects move in opposite directions, their separation rate is the sum of speeds: 50 + 70 = 120 km/h. Time = 360 / 120 = 3 hours. Always add speeds for opposite directions, subtract for same direction.

## Q6
**difficulty:** Easy
**type:** Problem Solving
**topic:** Two Objects Moving

Tom leaves home jogging at 6 km/h. Thirty minutes later, his sister leaves the same point walking at 4 km/h in the opposite direction. How far apart are they 1 hour after Tom left home?

- A) 4 km
- B) 5 km
- C) 6 km
- D) 8 km
- E) 10 km

**answer:** D
**explanation:** In 1 hour, Tom has traveled 6 x 1 = 6 km. His sister has been walking only 0.5 hour, covering 4 x 0.5 = 2 km. Since they're going opposite ways, total separation = 6 + 2 = 8 km.

## Q7
**difficulty:** Medium
**type:** Problem Solving
**topic:** Average Rate / Harmonic Mean

A driver travels from town X to town Y at 40 km/h and returns along the same road at 60 km/h. What is the average speed for the entire round trip?

- A) 46 km/h
- B) 48 km/h
- C) 50 km/h
- D) 52 km/h
- E) 55 km/h

**answer:** B
**explanation:** Never average speeds directly — that gives the trap 50. When distances are equal, use the harmonic mean: 2ab/(a+b) = 2(40)(60)/(40+60) = 4800/100 = 48 km/h. Alternatively, let d = 120 km each way: total distance 240, total time 3 + 2 = 5, average = 48.

## Q8
**difficulty:** Medium
**type:** Problem Solving
**topic:** Two Objects Moving

A freight train leaves station P at 8:00 AM traveling east at 45 mph. A passenger train leaves the same station at 10:00 AM traveling east at 65 mph. At what time does the passenger train catch the freight train?

- A) 2:30 PM
- B) 4:00 PM
- C) 4:30 PM
- D) 5:30 PM
- E) 6:00 PM

**answer:** A
**explanation:** Catch-up problems use the difference in speeds. By 10:00 AM, the freight train is 2 x 45 = 90 miles ahead. Closing rate = 65 - 45 = 20 mph. Time to catch = 90/20 = 4.5 hours after the passenger train departs. 10:00 AM + 4.5 hours = 2:30 PM.

## Q9
**difficulty:** Medium
**type:** Problem Solving
**topic:** Combined Work

Three identical pumps working together can drain a pool in 4 hours. How long would it take 5 such pumps to drain the pool?

- A) 2 hours
- B) 2 hours 24 minutes
- C) 2 hours 40 minutes
- D) 3 hours
- E) 3 hours 20 minutes

**answer:** B
**explanation:** Total work = pumps x time = 3 x 4 = 12 pump-hours. With 5 pumps: time = 12/5 = 2.4 hours = 2 hours 24 minutes. Inverse proportionality: more pumps, less time.

## Q10
**difficulty:** Medium
**type:** Problem Solving
**topic:** Distance-Rate-Time

A commuter drives 60 miles to work. If she increases her usual speed by 10 mph, she arrives 15 minutes earlier. What is her usual speed in mph?

- A) 30
- B) 35
- C) 40
- D) 45
- E) 50

**answer:** C
**explanation:** Let r = usual speed. Usual time = 60/r; faster time = 60/(r+10). The difference is 1/4 hour: 60/r - 60/(r+10) = 1/4. Multiply through by 4r(r+10): 240(r+10) - 240r = r(r+10), so 2400 = r^2 + 10r. Solving r^2 + 10r - 2400 = 0 gives r = 40 (reject negative root).

## Q11
**difficulty:** Medium
**type:** Problem Solving
**topic:** Two Objects Moving

Two friends live 48 km apart. They start biking toward each other at the same time — one at 14 km/h and the other at 10 km/h. How long until they meet?

- A) 1 hour 30 minutes
- B) 1 hour 45 minutes
- C) 2 hours
- D) 2 hours 15 minutes
- E) 2 hours 30 minutes

**answer:** C
**explanation:** Moving toward each other, the closing rate is the sum: 14 + 10 = 24 km/h. Meeting time = distance / closing rate = 48/24 = 2 hours.

## Q12
**difficulty:** Medium
**type:** Problem Solving
**topic:** Combined Work

Worker A can complete a job in 12 hours. When A and B work together, they finish the same job in 8 hours. How long would B take working alone?

- A) 16 hours
- B) 20 hours
- C) 24 hours
- D) 30 hours
- E) 36 hours

**answer:** C
**explanation:** Use 1/a + 1/b = 1/T. Here 1/12 + 1/b = 1/8. So 1/b = 1/8 - 1/12 = 3/24 - 2/24 = 1/24. Therefore b = 24 hours. Classic trap: adding times (12 + 8 = 20) is answer B.

## Q13
**difficulty:** Medium
**type:** Problem Solving
**topic:** Average Rate / Harmonic Mean

Anna drives the first 100 miles of a trip at 50 mph and the last 150 miles at 75 mph. What is her average speed for the entire trip?

- A) 60 mph
- B) 62.5 mph
- C) 65 mph
- D) 66.67 mph
- E) 70 mph

**answer:** B
**explanation:** Harmonic mean doesn't apply here because distances differ. Use total distance / total time. Time 1 = 100/50 = 2 hr; time 2 = 150/75 = 2 hr. Total distance = 250 miles, total time = 4 hours, average = 250/4 = 62.5 mph. Always go back to total/total when legs are unequal.

## Q14
**difficulty:** Medium
**type:** Problem Solving
**topic:** Work with Different Rates

Alex starts painting a fence alone at a rate that would finish the job in 10 hours. After 4 hours, Beth joins him and together they finish the remaining work in 3 hours. How long would Beth take to paint the fence alone?

- A) 5 hours
- B) 6 hours
- C) 7.5 hours
- D) 10 hours
- E) 15 hours

**answer:** D
**explanation:** In 4 hours alone, Alex completes 4/10 = 2/5 of the fence, leaving 3/5. Together they finish that 3/5 in 3 hours, so combined rate = (3/5)/3 = 1/5 per hour. Set 1/10 + 1/b = 1/5, so 1/b = 1/5 - 1/10 = 1/10. Beth alone takes 10 hours.

## Q15
**difficulty:** Hard
**type:** Problem Solving
**topic:** Two Objects Moving

A boat travels 24 km downstream in 2 hours and returns upstream over the same distance in 3 hours. What is the speed of the boat in still water?

- A) 8 km/h
- B) 9 km/h
- C) 10 km/h
- D) 11 km/h
- E) 12 km/h

**answer:** C
**explanation:** Let b = boat speed, c = current. Downstream speed = b + c = 24/2 = 12. Upstream speed = b - c = 24/3 = 8. Adding: 2b = 20, so b = 10 km/h (and c = 2 km/h). Standard stream-current setup: add for downstream, subtract for upstream.

## Q16
**difficulty:** Hard
**type:** Problem Solving
**topic:** Work with Different Rates

Pipe X can fill a tank in 6 hours and pipe Y can fill it in 9 hours. Pipe X is opened first; 2 hours later Y is also opened. How long in total does it take from the moment X was first opened until the tank is full?

- A) 3.6 hours
- B) 4 hours
- C) 4.4 hours
- D) 4.8 hours
- E) 5.2 hours

**answer:** C
**explanation:** In the first 2 hours, X fills 2/6 = 1/3 of the tank, leaving 2/3. Combined rate after Y joins = 1/6 + 1/9 = 3/18 + 2/18 = 5/18 per hour. Time to fill remaining 2/3 = (2/3) / (5/18) = (2/3)(18/5) = 12/5 = 2.4 hours. Total time = 2 + 2.4 = 4.4 hours.

## Q17
**difficulty:** Hard
**type:** Data Sufficiency
**topic:** Distance-Rate-Time

A car traveled from city M to city N. What was its average speed for the trip?

(1) The distance from M to N is 180 miles.
(2) The trip took 20 minutes less than it would have at 45 mph.

- A) Statement (1) ALONE is sufficient, but statement (2) ALONE is not sufficient.
- B) Statement (2) ALONE is sufficient, but statement (1) ALONE is not sufficient.
- C) BOTH statements TOGETHER are sufficient, but NEITHER statement ALONE is sufficient.
- D) EACH statement ALONE is sufficient.
- E) Statements (1) and (2) TOGETHER are NOT sufficient.

**answer:** C
**explanation:** Statement (1) alone: distance only, no time — insufficient. Statement (2) alone: a relative time savings with no anchor to distance — insufficient. Combined: at 45 mph, time would be 180/45 = 4 hours. Actual time = 4 - 1/3 = 11/3 hours, so average speed = 180 / (11/3) = 540/11 mph — a single value. Sufficient together.

## Q18
**difficulty:** Hard
**type:** Data Sufficiency
**topic:** Combined Work

Two workers, P and Q, together complete a job. How many hours did they work together?

(1) Working alone, P takes 10 hours and Q takes 15 hours to complete the job.
(2) The job required 6 worker-hours of total effort from P and Q combined.

- A) Statement (1) ALONE is sufficient, but statement (2) ALONE is not sufficient.
- B) Statement (2) ALONE is sufficient, but statement (1) ALONE is not sufficient.
- C) BOTH statements TOGETHER are sufficient, but NEITHER statement ALONE is sufficient.
- D) EACH statement ALONE is sufficient.
- E) Statements (1) and (2) TOGETHER are NOT sufficient.

**answer:** A
**explanation:** Statement (1) alone: combined rate = 1/10 + 1/15 = 3/30 + 2/30 = 1/6, so working together they take 6 hours. Sufficient. Statement (2) alone: "6 worker-hours" sums their individual contributions but doesn't pin down the elapsed time without their rates — insufficient. Answer is A.
